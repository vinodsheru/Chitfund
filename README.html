01

Overview

Kumar Chits Manager is a browser-based organiser dashboard for running chit fund groups — built entirely as a self-contained HTML file that works offline, requires no login, and stores nothing in the cloud.

A chit fund (also called a "chit" or "chitty") is a rotating savings scheme popular across South India. A group of members each contribute a fixed monthly amount into a common pool. Every month, one member "lifts" (receives) the entire pool. This continues until every member has lifted once.

This dashboard is designed specifically for the organiser — the person who runs the chit, collects contributions, disburses payouts, and earns a profit for their service. The organiser does not participate as a member; they manage the group and retain earnings from the spread between what is collected and what is paid out.

02

How It Works

The Kumar Chits model uses a declining contribution structure — unlike traditional chit funds where all members pay the same amount every month, here a member's monthly contribution decreases over time the longer they wait before lifting.

Core Principle: Members who lift early pay more in total. Members who lift later pay less — because their monthly "Not Lift" rate keeps declining. The organiser benefits from the gap between total collections and total payouts.
  • 1
    Setup: Organiser decides the chit value (e.g. ₹15,000), number of members (e.g. 10), and duration (e.g. 10 months). Each member is assigned a month in which they will lift.
  • 2
    Contributions calculated: The "If Lift" rate (paid the month a member lifts) is fixed. The "Not Lift" rate (paid every other month) starts equal to the "If Lift" rate in Month 1 and decreases by a fixed step each month.
  • 3
    Monthly pool: Each month, one member lifts. That member pays the "If Lift" rate. All other members pay the "Not Lift" rate for that month. The total collected is the pool.
  • 4
    Payout: The lifting member receives the full chit value (e.g. ₹15,000) regardless of which month they lift.
  • 5
    Organiser profit: Because the pool collected each month (especially early months) exceeds the fixed payout, the organiser accumulates a profit over the full duration.
Important: The organiser is not counted as a member. They do not lift a chit. Their income is purely the difference between what is collected from members and what is paid out as chit value.
03

The Formula

The contribution rates are derived from a precise formula verified across all reference chit plans from Kumar Chits. Every number shown in the dashboard matches exactly with the printed reference charts.

Contribution Rate Calculation
IF LIFT = round( ChitValue × 1.2 ÷ Members ) Fixed every month
NOT LIFT (Month 1) = IF LIFT Starts equal to lift rate
STEP = round( startAmt ÷ (2 × (N−1)) ) Monthly reduction, rounded clean
NOT LIFT (Month m) = startAmt − (m−1) × step Decreases each month
POOL (Month m) = (Members−1) × notLift(m) + ifLift Total collected that month
ORGANISER PROFIT = Σ Pool(m) − (N × ChitValue) Across all N months

Features

The dashboard is packed with organiser-focused tools. All features run entirely in your browser — no internet required once the file is opened.

⚙️
Flexible Configuration

Set any chit value, any number of members, and any duration. Supports chit values from ₹10,000 to ₹5,00,000 and durations from 2 to 50 months.

📐
Auto Formula Engine

Instantly calculates "If Lift", "Not Lift", step reduction, pool per month, and organiser profit — all matching the verified Kumar Chits formula.

✏️
Manual Overrides

Not happy with the calculated amounts? Edit any "Not Lift" or "If Lift" value for any month directly in the table. Overridden cells turn red; auto cells stay green.

↺
Reset to Auto

One button to discard all manual changes and revert every month's contribution back to the auto-calculated formula values.

👥
Member Management

Add and edit member names and phone numbers. Assign each member to their chosen lift month. The system flags conflicts if two members are assigned the same month.

📅
Calendar Integration

Set a chit start month (e.g. June 2025) and all months in the schedule automatically show real calendar month names (Jun 2025, Jul 2025…).

📊
Live Profit Tracking

The profit banner updates in real time as you change any value — showing total collected, total paid out, net organiser profit, and average monthly earnings.

📋
Member-wise Summary

For every member: total amount paid across all months, amount received, net gain or loss, effective chit cost, and savings compared to lifting in Month 1.

📉
Cumulative Balance

The schedule tab shows the organiser's cumulative running balance after each month — so you can see exactly when you break even and when profit builds.

⬇️
CSV / Excel Downloads

Download any table as a CSV file: Contribution Table, Monthly Schedule, Member Details, or a complete combined report — all open directly in Microsoft Excel or Google Sheets.

🌐
Works Offline

🔒
Private by Default

Your data never leaves your device. There is no backend, no analytics, no data collection. The file can be hosted privately on Netlify with password protection if needed.

Tab-by-Tab Guide

The dashboard is organised into five tabs. Each tab focuses on a specific aspect of chit management.

  • ①
    Setup Tab — Enter the chit value, number of members, duration, group name, and start month. Hit "Generate Chit Plan" and all five tabs populate instantly. The live formula bar shows the derived rates before you even generate. The profit banner at the bottom summarises total earnings.
  • ②
    Contributions Tab — Shows the full contribution table month-by-month: Not Lift rate, If Lift rate, pool collected, chit payout, and organiser gain per month. Every cell in the Not Lift and If Lift columns is editable — type in a new number to override. A running total row shows the grand figures.
  • ③
    Schedule Tab — Shows who lifts each calendar month, how much the pool is, how much is paid out, and the organiser's gain and running cumulative profit for each month. This is the primary record you'd use month-to-month during the chit run.
  • ④
    Members Tab — Lists all members with editable name and phone fields. A dropdown lets you change which month each member lifts. Columns show their total contribution, what they receive, and their net position.
  • ⑤
    Summary Tab — A clean final summary per member: total paid, received, net gain/loss, effective cost, and savings compared to lifting in Month 1. Plus an overall organiser profit card at the top. Best used for printing or sharing.

Quick Reference

Key terms used throughout the dashboard:

  • ◆
    Chit Value — The lump sum amount paid to the member who lifts in a given month (e.g. ₹15,000).
  • ◆
    If Lift — The contribution paid by a member in the specific month they lift. Fixed and equal to ChitValue × 1.2 ÷ Members.
  • ◆
    Not Lift — The contribution paid by a member in every month they do not lift. Starts equal to "If Lift" in Month 1 and decreases by the step amount each month.
  • ◆
    Step — The fixed monthly reduction in the "Not Lift" contribution rate, automatically calculated and rounded to a clean number.
  • ◆
    Pool — Total amount collected in a given month = (Members − 1) × notLift(m) + ifLift. This is always greater than the chit value — the difference is organiser profit.
  • ◆
    Organiser Profit — Cumulative surplus across all months = Total Collected − Total Paid Out. The organiser is not a member and earns only this profit.
  • ◆
    Manual Override — When you edit any "Not Lift" or "If Lift" cell directly, that cell is flagged as manually set (shown with red border) and excluded from auto-recalculation until reset.
File tech stack: Pure HTML + CSS + JavaScript. No frameworks, no npm, no build step. Any browser from the past 5 years can run it. File size is under 50 KB.
